# WORTH

> **You don't owe a number on a scale your dating life.** Worth is the coach that uncouples your value from your weight loss timeline.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You decided a while ago that you'd start dating when you lose the weight. You set a number, told yourself it was just practical, and put your romantic life on a shelf. The number keeps moving. You hit it, set a new one, then stall, then gain some back, and the shelf keeps gaining weight too. You haven't been on a date in years. You're not unhappy with your life, but you're aware that you're postponing a real part of it on a condition that may never neatly arrive. The diet apps are no help, they reinforce the contract. The dating apps don't care about your inner monologue. You know intellectually that your worth isn't a number. You don't actually believe it yet, and no algorithm has tried to help you believe it.

**Target users**

Primary: adults who've been actively losing weight for six-plus months and have explicitly told themselves they'll date when they hit a target. Skews 25-40, skews women.

Not for: people who genuinely don't want to date right now, or people whose dating gap is unrelated to body image. Worth is for the conditional-on-weight crowd.

## 2. Product experience

Worth is a small, deliberate course of work, not an endless app. You enter at week one and the first thing it does is ask you to write down the rule you've been living by: "I'll date when I weigh X." Just seeing it written down, with the date you set the rule, is often the first crack in it.

From there Worth runs a six-week sequence. Each week introduces a small piece of work: a prompt to identify what you actually want from dating, a separation exercise between your body and your worth, a stretch task like reactivating a profile or texting a friend's friend, a reflection on what came up. The tasks are sized for emotional weight, not time. Some weeks you do five minutes. Some weeks you do something that takes courage.

Worth measures progress on its own axis. Not pounds, not matches, not dates. It tracks how much of your dating freeze is still in place, by asking you a small set of questions each week. Over six weeks the curve bends. Most users don't end the program in a relationship. They end it having had one conversation, one coffee, one moment where their worth was visible to them outside of a weigh-in.

The app is also explicit about what it isn't. It is not a body-positivity app. It is not a dating app. It is not anti-weight-loss. You can keep losing weight if you want. Worth's only argument is that the dating freeze and the weight loss are two separate things, and that decoupling them is the move that actually changes your life.

In short: Worth is a six-week course that gets you back into your dating life without waiting for the scale to give you permission.
