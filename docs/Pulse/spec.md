## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You love your partner. The sex part is harder than it used to be, or harder than you would want, and neither of you knows how to bring it up without it becoming a thing. One of you wants more, one of you wants different, one of you wants to be wanted in a way that has stopped happening. You have tried the late-night vulnerable version and it spiralled into a fight. You have tried the casual joke and it landed wrong. You have tried not bringing it up at all and that is going worse. Reading about it on your own helps a little, but the books were written for couples who are already talking, and you and your partner cannot find the door into the conversation. So it sits, quietly mattering more every month, and the gap between what you each want and what either of you knows about the other widens.

**Target users**

Primary: established couples, 25-45, who love each other and have hit a wall on intimacy conversations, not crisis couples and not new couples. The wedge is the partner who has been doing the reading: the one who already knows the conversation needs to happen and is looking for the format that makes it possible.

Not for: single people looking for sex advice, or couples in crisis who need a therapist. Pulse is a structured conversation, not therapy and not content.

## 2. Product experience

Pulse is a shared room for the conversation neither of you has been able to start.

It begins with one of you. You download Pulse, set it up, and invite your partner with a link. The framing matters: Pulse is not your tool that you are bringing to them, it is a shared space you are both walking into. Until your partner accepts, nothing happens. When they do, Pulse opens its first session for both of you.

A session is a guided exchange, not a chat. Pulse asks each of you the same question, separately, and you each answer privately. The questions start very small, something you appreciated about them this week, something that felt good, a memory of an early moment. They get more honest over time, on your timeline, never forced. After you both answer, Pulse shows you each other's answers side by side, and gives you a short, gentle prompt for the conversation it just opened. The whole thing takes ten minutes. Most of the work happens after, in the kitchen, on the walk, in bed, with the real conversation Pulse made possible.

The sessions build. Pulse holds the thread across weeks, remembers what you have already named and what you have not, and slowly moves into the harder territory only when both of you have shown you are ready. Desire mismatches, fantasies neither of you has said out loud, the thing one of you stopped doing and the other one misses, the question of what wanting each other looks like five years in. Nothing is forced. If either of you pauses, the app waits, no streak, no nag.

Pulse refuses to be a sex content app. There is no library of positions, no quiz that scores your relationship, no community feed of other couples. It is one private room with two chairs, a steady voice, and a slow set of doors. The bet is that almost every couple already knows what they want to say, they just need a format where saying it does not blow up the night.

In short: Pulse opens the conversation you both want, and stays out of the way while you have it.
