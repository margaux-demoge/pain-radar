# HELD

> **Touch is a basic human need, and nobody talks about not getting any.** Held finds the people near you who are missing it too, and gets you in a room together.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You have not been hugged in a long time. Not in the romantic sense, in the basic human sense. You live alone, or you live with people who do not touch you, and the last time someone held you for more than two seconds was a goodbye at the airport six months ago. You are not lonely in the way the word usually means, you have friends, you have a life, you do not need a partner. You need the physical thing that bodies need. Talking about it is hard because the culture has only one shelf for "needs physical contact" and it is the romantic one, so you make a joke about it and move on. The closest thing to a fix is paying someone, which costs money and feels off, or signing up for cuddle puddles you have read about in articles and never quite trusted, or just enduring it. The basic need keeps not getting met, and the absence of it shows up everywhere else in your nervous system.

**Target users**

Primary: adults 25-45 living alone or in low-touch households, in cities where the cultural script for platonic physical contact is thin. The wedge is the person who has already named the need to themselves, but cannot find an honest, safe, non-romantic place to meet it.

Not for: anyone looking for romantic or sexual contact. Held has zero tolerance for that drift and is designed to make it structurally impossible.

## 2. Product experience

Held is the way you find the people near you who are also missing touch, and end up in the same room.

You open Held and the first thing it does is check that you understand the deal. Held is platonic, fully clothed, structured, in groups, never one on one for first meetings, and the safety scaffolding is the product, not a footnote. You verify your identity. You read and accept the consent framework. You are asked a few honest questions about what you want and what you do not want, and the answers shape what Held shows you.

Then you find an event. Held organises small, hosted gatherings in real spaces, eight to twelve people, a trained facilitator, a clear two-hour structure: arrival and conversation, guided exercises that build comfort, a held space where people can ask for and offer a hug, a head on a shoulder, a hand on a back, leaning against another person while everyone listens to music. Nobody is touched without explicit asking, nobody is pressured to give, the facilitator is watching, and the boundaries are visible in the room. You leave when you want. You can choose not to participate in any part.

After your first event you can attend others, and over time you start recognising faces. Some of the people you meet become acquaintances, some become friends, some you only ever see at Held, and that is fine, the point was never to build a network. The point was to get the touch your body needed and to do it in a setting that is safe enough that your nervous system actually relaxed into it. You can also offer to host or volunteer as a steward once you have attended enough.

Held is structurally not a dating app and works hard to stay that way. There are no DMs in-product, no profiles to scroll, no photos to swipe on. The only meaningful action is RSVPing to an event. If a participant tries to convert the space into something romantic or sexual, they are removed. The slowness and the friction are the product, not bugs to fix.

In short: you book the event, you show up, you get the touch your body has been missing, in a room where that is the whole point.
