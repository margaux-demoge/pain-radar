## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You want to take care of your skin. You opened r/SkincareAddiction once and three years later you have a shelf of products, a routine in twelve steps, and no idea if any of it is actually working. Every time you read a new thread you add another acid, another serum, another sunscreen, and your skin keeps doing the same thing it was doing before. Or worse, it gets irritated and you can't tell which of the twelve products is the culprit. You don't want to be a skincare expert. You want to know the three things you should put on your face, in what order, for the next ninety days, and to be told to ignore everything else. Nobody on the internet will tell you that, because everyone selling you skincare has an interest in adding to your routine, not subtracting from it.

**Target users**

Primary: adults who care about their skin but feel paralyzed by the choices. They've spent money, watched Hyram, scrolled SkincareAddiction, and ended up overwhelmed rather than informed.

Not for: skincare hobbyists who enjoy the complexity, or people with diagnosed conditions that need a dermatologist. Bare is for the overwhelmed normal person who just wants a clean answer.

---

## 2. Product experience

Bare opens with a short, honest quiz. Skin type, current state, three things you're hoping to change, what you've tried, what irritated you. The output isn't a personalized routine of nine products. It's three: a cleanser, a treatment, a sunscreen, named by ingredient and price tier, not by brand promo deal. Three is the answer for the first ninety days, no matter what.

Every morning and evening Bare gives you the two-tap routine and logs that you did it. Once a week it asks one question: how is your skin doing, on a 1 to 5, and shows you a side-by-side photo if you want to take one. That's the whole feedback loop.

When something isn't working after three weeks Bare doesn't add a product, it swaps one. The treatment is the most likely thing to change, and Bare walks you through why. It teaches you, in one sentence at a time, the actual ingredient that's doing the work, so you stop being a captive to brand marketing and start being a person with a routine you understand.

The hardest thing Bare does is hold the line. When you read about a new acid on TikTok and want to add it, the app says no. When influencer season hits with the next must-have serum, the app says no. The product's job is to protect you from your own urge to add complexity, until your skin has actually told you what works. The reward is a face that looks better, in less time, with less spent.

In short: Bare gives you a three-product routine and refuses to let you add to it until your skin has earned the next move.
