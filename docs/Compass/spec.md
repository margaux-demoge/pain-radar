# COMPASS

> **The map between cold feet and real incompatibility.** Compass tells you which differences are growth edges and which ones are the door.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You love them, mostly. But there are these things. They want kids and you're not sure. You're ambitious and they're content. They want to live in their hometown, you're already imagining the move. The differences are real and they don't go away when you bring them up. Your friends are split, half say "every couple has these, work on it", half say "those are dealbreakers, you're wasting time". Generic advice is useless because it doesn't know which of your differences matter and which don't, and you've stopped trusting your own gut because you've been with them three years and your gut is biased. You need a clearer way to see which gaps are the door and which are just life.

**Target users**

Primary: 26-36 year olds in serious relationships (1-5 years) facing real value/lifestyle differences with their partner, often pre-engagement or pre-major-life-step (move, kids, marriage). Both partners often suspect the answer; neither wants to say it first. They want a rigorous, private process, not couples therapy and not Tarot.

Not for: couples in the first year (too early), or couples where one partner has already decided to leave (Compass is for genuine ambiguity, not for justifying decisions already made).

## 2. Product experience

Compass starts with a 30-minute alone-time exercise that's deliberately uncomfortable. You map your non-negotiables, the things you cannot give up without becoming a different person. Not "I want a partner who's kind", real specifics: kids by 35, never living more than two hours from my mother, a certain financial trajectory, monogamy, faith. Then you map your preferences, the things you'd like but could trade. Then your unknowns, the things you genuinely haven't decided.

When you're done, Compass asks you to do the same exercise for your partner, what you believe they would say. The gap between your guess and the truth is where most of the work happens. You'll be wrong about them in places you didn't expect. Often your partner has flexed on something you'd been treating as their absolute, or they're firmer than you thought on something you'd been dismissing.

Then, optionally, your partner does the exercise themselves, in their own session, no peeking. Compass surfaces only the points of friction, the items where your non-negotiables don't align, with no judgment on who's right. The output isn't a compatibility score, it's a short list: "you both have non-negotiables here, here, and here, and they conflict. These are the conversations you actually need to have." That's it. The app gets out of the way and the humans take it from there.

If you're not ready to do the joint version, you can stay solo. Compass will help you sit with your own findings, ask the harder follow-ups, and over weeks you can see whether the picture changes. Sometimes "cold feet" turns into "this is the door" once you can name the actual mismatch. Sometimes it turns into "this was a story I was telling myself" once you see your non-negotiables don't actually conflict where you thought they did.

In short: stop arguing about whether to stay or go. See the actual map, name the actual conflicts, and decide from clarity instead of fog.
