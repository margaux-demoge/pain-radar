## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You've been losing weight, doing the work, eating less, moving more, and one morning the scale stops cooperating. A week passes, then two, and the number won't budge. You start to spiral. You wonder if your body is broken, if you've been kidding yourself, if the last forty pounds were a fluke and you're about to undo all of it. Every weigh-in feels like a verdict. The apps you use only know how to celebrate a downward line, so when the line goes flat they have nothing to say, and the silence reads as failure. You start to consider whether to give up entirely, not because the goal changed but because the scale told you a story and you believed it.

**Target users**

Primary: adults six to eighteen months into an active weight loss journey who have already lost meaningful weight and have now stalled. They are calorie-aware, they weigh themselves often, and they treat the scale as the only honest signal. They post in r/loseit when they get scared.

Not for: people starting from zero or people who have made peace with their body and aren't trying to change it. Crest is for the messy middle, not the start and not the finish.

## 2. Product experience

Crest is a coach that takes over exactly when your weight loss app gives up. The first time you open it you tell it what you've been doing, how long you've been at it, what your weight curve looks like, and where the plateau hit. From that moment Crest stops caring about the daily scale number as the headline. Instead it pulls back and reframes what's happening.

Every morning you weigh in like you always have, but Crest doesn't show you the single number. It shows you the trend line over fourteen days, with a calm explanation of what's actually moving and what isn't. If you're plateaued, it tells you why plateaus happen biologically and where you sit in the most common timelines, so you stop reading the flat line as personal failure.

The second thing Crest does is shift attention onto non-scale victories. Once a week it walks you through a short reflection: how your clothes fit, how your sleep feels, the lift you can do now that you couldn't three months ago, the photo comparison. Each one is logged and stitched into a parallel curve, so you can see the version of progress the scale refuses to show you.

When a real plateau persists past three weeks, Crest moves into troubleshooter mode. It walks you through the small list of things that actually matter, recalibrated maintenance calories, a diet break, a refeed week, a sleep audit, and helps you pick one to try, not five. It is not a meal plan and not a workout app. It is the thing you wish you had standing next to you when the scale stopped moving and you started doubting everything.

In short: when the scale stops moving, Crest shows you the progress the scale can't see and tells you exactly what to try next.
