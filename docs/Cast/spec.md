# CAST

> **The man, woman, or person you actually want to be.** Cast helps you pick the traits, drop the ones that aren't yours, and live in the version that fits.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You know what kind of man, or woman, or person you are supposed to be, you just are not sure you want to be it. The traditional script feels too narrow, the progressive script feels too vague, the internet gives you a hundred versions of strong and feminine and masculine and soft and none of them are obviously you. You catch yourself trying on traits like clothes, the stoic provider one day, the emotionally open one the next, the hyper-feminine performer on Sunday, the no-makeup minimalist on Monday, and somewhere underneath it all you are tired of performing and not sure what is left when you stop. Asking anyone directly feels embarrassing, so you ask the internet, and the internet sends you to a forum where someone is asking the same question and getting roasted for it.

**Target users**

Primary: people 18-30 actively renegotiating their gender presentation, identity, or role, in the quiet way that most people do, not as a public coming-out but as a private rewrite. The wedge is the curious self-examiner: the person who already reads about masculinity or femininity or identity online and is trying to assemble their own version without committing to a tribe.

Not for: people seeking medical transition support, or anyone looking for community validation. Cast is a private workshop, not a forum.

## 2. Product experience

Cast is a quiet space to figure out what version of yourself actually fits.

You start by telling Cast where you are. Not who you are, where you are. What feels off right now, what you have been trying on, what you used to do that you do not want to do anymore. There is no quiz that pegs you as a type, no spectrum slider that puts you on a graph. Cast listens, asks a few real questions, and shows you back the picture it is building: the traits you seem drawn to, the ones you are reacting against, the contradictions you are sitting with.

Then it gives you the workshop. You can pull up any trait, traditionally masculine restraint, traditionally feminine softness, traditionally androgynous independence, whatever vocabulary you brought, and Cast lets you examine it honestly. What does it look like when it is healthy in someone, what does it look like when it is performative, what does it cost, what does it give. You can try it on for a week, a small daily prompt that nudges you to live in that trait for seven days, and Cast asks you at the end what stuck, what felt fake, what you want to keep.

Over weeks Cast builds your portrait, not a label, a collection of traits that you have actively chosen rather than inherited. Some of them are traditional, some are not, none of them have to make a coherent ideology, they only have to be yours. You can revisit and revise the portrait whenever you want. The point is not to land somewhere, the point is to stop pretending the question is settled when it is not.

Cast deliberately avoids prescribing. It will not tell you that real men do this or that real women feel that. It will not push you toward or away from any tradition. It is a mirror with a notepad, not a coach with an opinion. Nothing is shared, nothing is posted, no community sees your portrait.

In short: you bring the question of who to be, Cast helps you assemble the answer that is actually yours.
