## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You have been learning Spanish for eight months. You know the verb tables, you can read a news article slowly, and you have never once held a conversation. Duolingo gives you streaks but never gives you a person. A tutor costs forty dollars an hour and you can't justify three a week. You tried Tandem and HelloTalk and got a dozen "hey beautiful" messages within an hour, half of them clearly looking for something other than language practice. You go quiet for a month, your streak dies, and when you finally open your textbook again you have lost the words you already had. The thing standing between you and fluency is not vocabulary, it is a regular human on the other side of a call, and there is nowhere safe to find one.

**Target users**

Primary: adult learners (25-45) past the absolute beginner stage, working on one specific language with a clear motivation (partner's family, upcoming move, work). They have used at least one solo learning app and hit the speaking plateau. They are willing to commit to a schedule but not to a paid tutor.

Not for: tourists cramming for a one-week trip, polyglots collecting languages, or under-18 learners (safety surface is too risky).

## 2. Product experience

Lex is a matching app for one thing only: regular spoken practice with someone learning the mirror of what you are learning.

Onboarding is short and pointed. You pick the language you are learning, the language you speak fluently, your level on a five-step scale, your timezone, and two or three weekly slots where you can show up. Lex matches you with someone whose pair is the inverse of yours, whose level is within one step, and whose available slots overlap with yours. The first match arrives within 24 hours. There is no swipe deck and no chat-first phase, the match is the call slot.

The first call is scheduled, ten minutes, with a topic card on the screen. Half the time runs in your target language, half in theirs. The topic card gives both of you something to talk about so the silence doesn't kill you in minute two. After the call, both sides rate the session on a single axis: did this person show up and try. Two no-shows or two low ratings and the match dissolves quietly, no drama, you are re-matched.

The streak is shared, not personal. If you and your partner both complete your weekly calls, the streak holds for both of you. If one of you misses, both streaks pause. This is the lever: you are not accountable to an app, you are accountable to a person who is counting on you the same way you are counting on them. The streak is visible only between the two of you, not public, not gamified outside the pair.

Async lives in the gaps. Between calls you can send short voice messages, 30 seconds each, in your target language, and your partner sends one back. No text chat, no photos, no emojis. The constraint is the product: every interaction is speaking practice or it doesn't happen. The community is intentionally pair-shaped, not feed-shaped, so the safety profile of Tandem and HelloTalk (open DMs, photo-led profiles, mixed intent) does not apply.

In short: Lex matches you with one person learning the inverse of what you are learning, you call each other twice a week, and the streak you share is the reason you both keep showing up.

## 3. Hypotheses and success metrics

| #   | Hypothesis                                                                                                          | Metric                                                                                  | Target |
| --- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------ |
| H1  | The pair structure produces a real conversation partner, not a ghost.                                              | % of matched pairs that complete at least 2 calls in week 1.                            | 50%    |
