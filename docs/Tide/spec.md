## 1. Overview

**Problem**

Some days the world is fine and some days it is not, and you cannot always tell why. You snap at someone you love on a Tuesday and feel like a stranger to yourself by Thursday. You read a text three times and decide it was hostile, then read it a week later and it is obviously not. You get a day of brain fog and write yourself off as lazy. Somewhere underneath all of this, your cycle is moving, hormones are shifting, and the version of you reading the world today is not the version that read it last week. You know this in the abstract. The cycle trackers you have used told you when your period is coming, which you can already feel, and not much else. They did not tell you that the spiral you had on day 22 is the same spiral you had on day 22 last month, or that the brain fog has a name and a phase and a reason.

**Target users**

Primary: women 22-35 who menstruate and who already track their cycle loosely, but who are looking for psychological insight, not fertility prediction. The wedge is the introspective journaller: the person who already writes about their mood and wants someone to connect the dots they cannot see from inside.

Not for: people optimizing for conception or contraception. Tide is a mood lens on the cycle, not a medical product.

## 2. Product experience

Tide turns the cycle from a calendar into a mirror.

You open it once a day and answer two or three small questions about how you actually feel right now. Not a mood emoji on a five point scale, real questions: did your brain feel sharp or smudged today, did small things land harder than usual, did you want to be around people or alone. It takes thirty seconds. Tide already knows where you are in your cycle from a few minimum inputs you gave at the start, and over the first month it starts learning what your version of each phase looks like.

Then it tells you. Every morning a single short read: "you are on day 18, last cycle this is when you started feeling untouchable, this cycle the pattern looks similar so far". Or "you are on day 22, this is historically your hardest stretch, the things bothering you today are likely sharper than they will feel next week". The point is never to dismiss what you feel, it is to give you the timeline you cannot see from the inside, so the feeling stops being a verdict on your life and starts being a piece of weather.

Over time Tide builds your cycle profile, the shape of your luteal phase, the day your energy peaks, the stretch where conflict lands hardest. It surfaces them as patterns you can see, not as horoscopes. When something is unusual for you, this cycle is moodier than usual, your energy phase came late, it flags it gently. You can use those flags to plan: hold the hard conversation for next Tuesday, put the deep work on Thursday, do not make the big call today.

Tide does not sell you supplements, does not push a community feed, does not turn your hormones into a content stream. It is one quiet daily check-in and one quiet daily read, and the rest is in your hands. The bet is that once the pattern is visible, the cycle stops being a thing that happens to you and starts being a thing you live with.

In short: you log how you feel, Tide shows you why, and the next time it happens you see it coming.
