# PROSE

> **Dating that starts with what you wrote, not how you look.** Photos are blurred until you have exchanged real messages. You judge each other on words first.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

You poured effort into your Hinge prompts. You wrote things that were actually you, vulnerable, specific, funny. You picked the photos you thought said the right things about your life. And the pattern of what gets you matched is brutally clear: it is the photos. People swipe in 1.2 seconds, on the first image, before they read a single word. You feel reduced to a thumbnail. The conversations that do happen, when they happen, start from "hey." If you are not in the top photogenic decile, the app feels like a slot machine where the odds are visibly bad. If you are, the matches you get are people who liked your face, not your mind, and the dates show it.

**Target users**

Primary: 25-40 year olds who are good with words, value depth, and have hit the wall on photo-led dating apps. They include people in any photogenic range who are tired of being judged that way, and people who consistently date well in real life but underperform on dating apps.

Not for: users looking for purely visual or hookup-based matching, or anyone who finds the friction of writing more before seeing a face intolerable. Prose deliberately raises the writing bar.

## 2. Product experience

When you sign up to Prose, you upload photos like any dating app, but the photos start blurred and locked. You see other people's profiles as a name, an age, a location, and a written profile: their answers to three open-ended prompts, each between 80 and 300 characters. The prompts are designed for substance, not vibes. "What is something you changed your mind about?" "Tell me about a place you go when you need to think." "What is a friendship in your life that has lasted?"

You like a profile by writing the opening message. There is no "tap to like" with no message attached. The opening message has a 50-character minimum and goes straight to them. They see your profile, also still with photos locked, and decide whether to respond. If they respond, you have a conversation. After both of you have exchanged at least three messages each, the photos unblur, on both sides, at once.

The unblur is the commitment moment. By the time you see each other, you both already decided the person you are talking to is interesting. The photos confirm or end it, but they did not start it. If either side does not like what they see, they can close the chat with no awkward continuation.

Prose's matching is not based on photos or any visual signal. It uses the language of your profile and your messages, what topics you write about, how you write, what you respond to, to surface people you might actually connect with. The signal is what you say.

In short: dating that starts with writing, exposes the face only after you have earned a conversation, and matches you on substance.
