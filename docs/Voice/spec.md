## 1. Overview

**Problem**

There is something you need to say to someone in your life and you have been carrying it for weeks. Your friends keep making a joke that lands wrong. Your partner does a thing that quietly bothers you every day. Your mom needs to hear something hard. Your coworker crossed a line and you froze. You know what you feel, you just cannot find the version of the words that says it without blowing up the relationship, without sounding cold, without crying mid-sentence, without losing the point. So you keep not saying it. The discomfort builds, the resentment leaks out sideways in tone and silence, and the moment you finally open your mouth it comes out either too soft to register or too sharp to take back. The script you needed existed in your head, you just could not write it under pressure.

**Target users**

Primary: people 20-40 who are conflict-averse, emotionally articulate in writing but not in real time, and who care enough about the relationship to want to get the words right. The wedge is the person who drafts difficult texts five times before sending, and rehearses hard conversations in the shower.

Not for: people looking for assertiveness training or therapy. Voice writes the line, it does not fix the underlying pattern.

## 2. Product experience

Voice is the friend who is good with words, on call for the conversation you keep putting off.

You open the app when something is sitting on your chest. You tell Voice who the other person is to you, what they did or said, what you feel about it, and what you actually want to come out of the conversation. It can be typed or spoken, and Voice listens more than it asks. It is not trying to diagnose you, it is trying to understand the relationship and the stakes well enough to write something you would actually say.

Then it gives you the words. Not one bland template, three or four real versions, each with a different posture: the soft version that opens a door, the direct version that names the thing, the warm version that protects the relationship, the firm version that draws the line. Each one is short, each one sounds like a real person, and each one is editable. You pick the one closest to your voice and Voice helps you tune it, swap a phrase, soften an edge, sharpen the ask. By the time you are done, you have the message or the opening line, and you have rehearsed it enough that saying it out loud is no longer the hardest part.

Voice also helps you choose how to deliver it. Some things land better as a text. Some things have to be in person. Some things need a voice note because tone is the whole point. Voice will tell you, honestly, which one this is, and what to do if the other person reacts in the three ways they are most likely to react. You leave the app with the words and a small plan, not just a paragraph.

You can save the situations you have worked through. Over time Voice learns the relationships in your life, the friend who needs warmth, the parent who needs structure, the partner who needs directness, and the scripts get more yours and less generic. Nothing is shared and nothing is posted. The whole app is one private room where you rehearse the things you mean to say.

In short: you tell Voice what is hard to say, Voice gives you the words to say it.
