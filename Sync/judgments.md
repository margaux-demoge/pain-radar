# Sync — judges verdicts

## benchmark-scanner: `GRAVEYARD`

The "ethical / transparent dating app" pitch has been tried repeatedly: academic researchers (Leeds, U-Mich) explicitly call it a fantasy, hobbyist open-source clones (Humbble, SimpleDating) never reach scale, and well-funded contrarians (Thursday, Hapend) either shut down or got accused of the exact dark patterns they claimed to oppose. Meanwhile incumbents like Hinge already claim algorithm openness (Gale-Shapley) and Feeld owns the "radical transparency" niche. The recurring failure pattern is liquidity: dating apps need supply of potential matches, and ideological positioning does not solve cold-start, so users return to Tinder/Hinge regardless of how they feel about the algorithm. Sync needs a concrete answer to "why won't this die the same death."

**Top competitors:**
- Hinge — already publicly discloses its Gale-Shapley-based algorithm and positions as the 'anti-Tinder', occupying the trust angle at scale (active, Match Group)
- Feeld — owns the 'radical transparency' positioning for non-traditional dating, publishes a State of Dating safety report (active)
- OkCupid — long-standing questionnaire-based compatibility scoring, historically the 'algorithm you can see' brand (active, Match Group)
- SciMatch — explicitly markets 'no paywall, no upsells, no boosts', flat-pricing alternative (active but tiny)
- Humbble / SimpleDating — open-source, non-profit, fully transparent matching projects positioning against Bumble/Tinder (active in code, no meaningful user base)

## retention-validator: `STRUCTURAL_RETURN`

Dating is naturally recurring until a relationship forms (job recurs daily/weekly while single). SDT: compétence is weak (dashboard gives some progress signal but it's diagnostic not mastery), autonomie is strong (user controls preferences, sees the function, no hidden dial), lien social is strong (matching/messaging is the core). Habit loop has an internal cue (boredom/loneliness/end-of-day swipe ritual seen in dating apps) and immediate reward (matches, messages). Two of three angles clearly pass (recurring job + habit loop with internal cue + 2 strong SDT needs), making this STRUCTURAL_RETURN. Main residual risk: the transparency wedge is a positioning differentiator, not the retention driver — retention rides on the same swipe loop as incumbents.

## user-interview-simulator: `BUILT_ON_SAND`

Sync positions on algorithm transparency, but the target user (a Tinder veteran frustrated by throttling) overwhelmingly cares about outcomes (matches, dates) not mechanism. The spec assumes "transparency = trust = signups" but provides no evidence users will switch ecosystems for a principle, especially to a cold-start app with no liquidity in their metro. Persona was constructible (clear: 25-40, dating-app fatigued, has read the discourse), so not FATAL, but the dangerous-assumption layer is mostly unanswered: liquidity, gameability of a published algorithm, and the leap from "I'm frustrated with Tinder" to "I will install an unknown app and rebuild my profile." Simulated interview, persona Léa, 31, Lyon, currently on Hinge + occasional Bumble, deleted Tinder twice, reads /r/Tinder threads, mildly cynical. Q2 "I don't actually want to read a ranking function, I want dates, what's your match volume in Lyon today?" UNANSWERED (no liquidity story). Q4 "I pay Hinge 20 euros a month and it kind of works, why is your flat sub better than just paying for a boost when I want one?" PARTIAL (flat sub stated, value not defended). Q5 "Your dashboard tells me my profile got 12 views this week, what am I supposed to do with that, the data doesn't get me a date?" PARTIAL (legibility claimed, no action loop). Q6 "Monthly transparency report, honestly who reads PDFs from a dating app?" UNANSWERED. Q7 "My friends are on Hinge, that's where the people I'd date actually are, how do you solve the empty-room problem?" UNANSWERED (cold start). The wedge (transparency) is intellectually appealing but the spec hasn't survived basic skeptical scrutiny on liquidity, verification, or outcome parity. H2 is the load-bearing claim (parity outcomes) and is entirely unvalidated.

**Dangerous assumptions:**
- Users actually care about algorithm mechanism enough to switch apps and rebuild a profile, rather than just wanting more matches from whichever app has the most people. Cheap test: show two landing-page variants to /r/Tinder readers, one selling transparency, one selling more matches, measure signup intent.
- A published ranking function can survive contact with adversarial users who will optimize their profiles to game it, without degrading match quality for everyone else. Cheap test: red-team the proposed ranking function with 5 growth-hacker types, see how trivially it inverts to a profile-gaming checklist.
- Sync can reach minimum dating-app liquidity in any single metro before users churn from the empty-room experience, with no engagement-maximization loop and no paid boosts to subsidize density. Cheap test: model required DAU per metro for a 2-matches-per-week median, then ask 20 target users what match volume they'd tolerate in week one before deleting.
