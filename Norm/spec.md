# NORM

> **Is this a phase or a flag?** Norm walks you through the behavior you're worried about and tells you, honestly, where it sits on the concern spectrum.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

Your kid did something today that's been bothering you. Maybe they hit their sibling, maybe they had a meltdown that lasted an hour, maybe they refuse to make eye contact with their grandparents. You don't know if it's a phase, a personality thing, a sign of something bigger, or just Tuesday. Your friends say "totally normal," your in-laws say "you need to discipline them more," and the internet has every answer at once. You don't want a parenting philosophy. You want someone who knows what's typical at this age and will tell you, calmly, whether what you saw today is in the range of normal or worth paying attention to. That conversation doesn't exist for free. Your pediatrician has eleven minutes, your therapist costs $200, and the forum says everything is autism.

**Target users**

Primary: parents of kids two to ten with one or two recurring behaviors that worry them, who have already googled it and come away more confused. They want a calm second opinion on the fly.

Not for: families already in clinical care, or parents looking for a parenting philosophy. Norm is the second opinion, not the system.

## 2. Product experience

Norm opens with a structured intake. You describe the behavior in plain words, when it happens, how often, and how your kid recovers from it. The app does the work of translating your description into the developmental language it knows: tantrums, transitions, sensory, social, attention, defiance. You don't need to know any of those words.

What you get back is calibrated. For your kid's age, here's the typical frequency and intensity of this behavior. Here's where yours falls. Here's what's generally helpful at this developmental stage. Here's the threshold where bringing it up with a pediatrician is the right move. The output is not a diagnosis, not a label, not a personality test. It's a calm sentence and a small picture of where you sit on the spectrum.

If you want, you can log the behavior over time. The next meltdown, the next refusal, with a tap. Norm starts building a small picture of how often it's actually happening, which is almost always less than you think. After three weeks you might find that the meltdown isn't every day, it's every five days, and that they happen mostly when there's a transition. That observation alone often solves the problem.

The thing Norm is hardest about is not amplifying. It will not surface diagnoses unless they're warranted. It will not show you forum threads. It will not push you toward services. It's a flat, honest read on a worry, delivered by an app that's incentivized only to be right, not to keep you scrolling.

In short: Norm tells you whether the behavior you're worried about is a phase or a flag, and gives you the threshold to act on.
