# PARE

> **Decide once, donate the rest.** Pare walks you through your closet item by item and makes the keep/donate call feel light instead of heavy.

## 1. Overview

**Problem**

Your closet is full. You wear the same fifteen pieces. The rest sits there, weighing on you every time you open the door. You've tried the Marie Kondo thing, the one-in-one-out rule, the hangers-turned-backward trick. Some of it worked for a week. Then you walked past a sweater that an ex bought you and froze, and the whole project stalled. The hardest part isn't the volume, it's the decision fatigue and the small sentimental weight on every single item. By the time you've considered ten things you're exhausted and you put everything back. Meanwhile the closet keeps growing, you keep buying, and you feel like a hoarder who can't even cosplay as a minimalist.

**Target users**

Primary: adults with too many clothes who have tried to declutter at least once and stalled. They feel guilt about waste, guilt about gifts, and guilt about money already spent.

Not for: people who already have minimal wardrobes or who genuinely enjoy a large rotating wardrobe. Pare is for the stuck declutterer.

## 2. Product experience

Pare is the calmest tinder for your closet. You photograph each piece, one at a time, and the app shows it to you alone, big, with one question underneath: keep or donate. There's no swiping a hundred items in a session. The app limits you to ten decisions a day on purpose, because decision fatigue is the actual blocker, not lack of time.

When you hesitate on a piece, Pare offers a second question that addresses the real reason. Did someone give this to you? Are you keeping it for the smaller version of yourself? Have you worn it in the last year? Each question is designed to surface the sentiment that's freezing the decision, name it, and then ask if that's a good enough reason to keep it. Most of the time, named out loud, it isn't.

After thirty days you have a small archive of donated pieces, photographed, with the reason you let them go. Pare keeps that archive deliberately. It is the proof you're not losing things, you're remembering them differently. When you donate, the app suggests a nearby drop-off or a postage-paid bag, and books it for you. The friction between deciding and getting the bag out of the house is where most declutters die.

Pare also tracks what's left, and starts showing you what you actually wear. After ninety days it can tell you that you've worn 22 pieces total, with photos. It makes the gap between what you own and what you use unignorable, in a soft way. The reward isn't a minimalist closet. It's a closet that doesn't weigh on you when you open it.

In short: Pare turns the impossible declutter into ten light decisions a day, and gets the donation bag out the door for you.
